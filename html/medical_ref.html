EMERGENCY #s

ADULT NORMAL VITALS

Heart Rate: 60–100 bpm  |  Blood Pressure: 90-120 / 60-80 mmHg
Respiratory Rate: 12–20 breaths/min  |  Temperature: 97.8–99.1°F (36.5–37.3°C)
Oxygen Saturation (SpO2): 95–100%  |  Blood Glucose (fasting): 70–100 mg/dL

PEDIATRIC VITALS (approximate)

Newborn HR: 100-160  |  Infant (1-12mo): 80-140  |  Toddler (1-3yr): 80-130
Child (3-10yr): 70-120  |  Pre-teen: 60-100
BP and RR also vary significantly by age — consult reference for specifics.

WHEN TO SEEK EMERGENCY CARE

Chest pain, difficulty breathing, sudden severe headache, facial drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty (stroke), uncontrolled bleeding, loss of consciousness, seizures, severe allergic reaction, suspected poisoning.

BLEEDING CONTROL

1. Apply direct pressure with clean cloth. 2. Elevate above heart if possible. 3. Do not remove cloth — add more on top. 4. Tourniquet for limb: 2-3" above wound, note time applied. 5. Seek medical care for deep wounds, spurting blood, or wounds that won't stop.

BURNS

Minor: cool running water 10-20 min. Do NOT use ice, butter, or toothpaste. Cover loosely. Severe: call 911. Do not pop blisters. Chemical: flush with water 20+ min. Electrical: don't touch victim if still in contact.

FRACTURES / SPRAINS

RICE: Rest, Ice (20 min on/off), Compression, Elevation. Splint fractures in position found. Do not straighten angulated fractures. Seek X-ray to differentiate sprain from fracture.

CHOKING — HEIMLICH

Adult conscious: stand behind, hands clasped above navel, below chest. Upward thrusts until dislodged. Infant: 5 back blows + 5 chest thrusts. Unconscious: CPR. If alone: use chair back or counter edge.

CPR — ADULT (Hands-Only)

1. Ensure scene safety. 2. Check responsiveness (tap/shout). 3. Call 911. 4. Hard, fast compressions center of chest, 2–2.4 inches deep. 5. Rate: 100-120/min (Stayin' Alive tempo). 6. Minimize interruptions. 7. Use AED as soon as available. Continue until EMS arrives.

CPR — INFANT

2 fingers on center of chest (just below nipple line). Depth: 1.5 inches. Rate: 100-120/min. 30 compressions : 2 rescue breaths if trained. Cover infant's mouth AND nose.

AED USE

1. Power on. 2. Attach pads (bare chest, dry). 3. Follow voice prompts. 4. Clear before shock. 5. Resume CPR immediately after shock. AEDs are designed for untrained users — let the device guide you.

WHAT TO TELL 911

1. Your location (address, cross streets, landmarks). 2. Nature of emergency. 3. Number of people injured. 4. Condition of patient. 5. Your phone number. Stay on the line — let dispatcher guide you.
